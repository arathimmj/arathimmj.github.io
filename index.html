Fort Kochi

Fort Kochi is a historic coastal area in the city of Kochi, Kerala, known for its colonial charm and cultural blend. With influences from Portuguese, Dutch, and British rule, the town has a distinct old-world feel, reflected in its architecture, streets, and art. It's a place where tradition and modern creativity meet—filled with galleries, heritage buildings, and a vibrant local vibe. Fort Kochi’s atmosphere is calm, artistic, and deeply rooted in history.
Activities:
  • Synagogue
  • Beach
  • Jew town
  • St. Francis Church
  • Dutch Palace
  • Chinese Fishing Net

Munnar

Munnar is a hill town in Kerala, located in the Western Ghats. Known for its cool climate and rolling tea plantations, it's a peaceful retreat surrounded by misty hills, valleys, and lush greenery. Once a summer resort for the British, Munnar blends colonial charm with the natural beauty of the high ranges. It is also home to a rich variety of flora and fauna, making it one of Kerala’s most serene and scenic regions.
Activities:
  • Eravikulam National park
  • Mattupetty Dam
  • Lockhart gap view point
  • Rose park
  • Tea trail
  • Spice garden visit
  • Echo point
  • Tea museum
  • Jeep trekking

Thekkady

Thekkady is a lush, hilly region in Kerala known for its cool climate, dense forests, and rich biodiversity. Surrounded by the Western Ghats, it’s filled with rolling hills, spice-scented plantations, and mist-covered valleys. The air is fresh, the landscape green, and the pace of life slow—making it a peaceful retreat into nature.
Activities:
  • Periyar Boating
  • Elephant Park
  • Bamboo Rafting
  • Traditional shows
  • Forest hiking

Alappuzha

Alappuzha is famous for its enchanting backwaters, houseboat cruises, and scenic canals, earning it the nickname “Venice of the East.” Glide through lush landscapes on a houseboat, explore traditional village life, or relax on Alappuzha Beach. Ideal for a peaceful escape into nature and culture.
Activities:
  • Boat House
  • Beach
  • Backwater
  • Light house and museum

Varkala

Varkala is a small coastal town in the southern part of Kerala, India, known for its relaxed atmosphere and natural beauty. It lies along the Arabian Sea and is unique for its high cliffs that run adjacent to the shoreline—a rare geological feature in Kerala. The town blends a traditional Kerala lifestyle with a slow-paced, bohemian vibe, attracting both locals and international visitors. It’s also known for its spiritual significance, wellness retreats, and as a peaceful getaway spot, reflecting the laid-back charm of coastal Kerala.
Activities:
  • Janardhana Swami Temple
  • Varkala Cliff

Athirapilly

Athirapilly is a breathtaking waterfall located in the Thrissur district of Kerala, India. Cascading from a height of about 80 feet, it is the largest waterfall in the state and is often referred to as the "Niagara of India." Surrounded by lush greenery and the Western Ghats, Athirapilly is a popular tourist destination known for its scenic beauty and biodiversity. The nearby Vazhachal Falls and the dense Sholayar forest add to its charm.
Activities:
  • Vazhachal Waterfalls
  • Trekking and Nature Walks
  • Thumboormuzhi Dam
  • Bamboo Rafting
